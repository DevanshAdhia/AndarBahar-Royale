AndarBahar Royale
AndarBahar Royale is a Python-based simulation of the popular Indian card game Andar Bahar, developed with an interactive Tkinter GUI. This project allows players to bet on either the Andar (inside) or Bahar (outside) side, watch cards being dealt alternately, and track their virtual balance in real-time. The game provides an engaging casino-like experience directly on your computer.
Features
Interactive GUI: Built with Tkinter for an intuitive and visually appealing interface.
Card Animation: Cards are dealt alternately between Andar and Bahar, simulating real gameplay.
Betting System: Players can place bets on Andar or Bahar with adjustable bet amounts (₹50, ₹100, ₹200).
Balance Tracking: Keep track of your balance, winnings, and losses throughout the game.
Game Validation: Ensures players cannot place bets without sufficient balance and handles new game initialization automatically.
Joker Card Display: Shows the initial joker card that determines the winning side.
Real-Time Gameplay: Cards are revealed sequentially with delays to mimic real dealing, enhancing player engagement.
How to Play
Launch the game by running the Python script.
The player starts with an initial balance of ₹1000.
Click New Game to shuffle the deck and reveal the joker card.
Choose to place your bet on Andar or Bahar.
The cards will be dealt alternately on each side until the card matching the joker’s value appears.
If the winning side matches your bet, you receive double your bet amount. If not, the bet is lost.
Adjust your bet amount by clicking Change Bet, cycling through ₹50, ₹100, and ₹200.
Continue playing until your balance runs out or as long as you like.
